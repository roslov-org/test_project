Privacy Policy

PureAir (demo) · Version 2026-06-19 · Last updated 19 June 2026

This is a demonstration privacy notice for the PureAir early-access waitlist. It explains what personal data we collect when you join the waitlist, why we collect it, and the rights you have.

Who we are

PureAir (the “demo store”) operates this landing page. For any privacy question or to exercise your rights, contact us at hello@pureair.example.

What we collect

  • Email address — the only personal field the waitlist form asks for.
  • Signup metadata — the time you signed up, the page/source, and a one-way hash of your IP address (we never store your raw IP).
  • Proof of consent — a record that you ticked the consent box, with the exact text and version of this policy shown to you at the time.

Why we collect it & legal basis

We use your email only to send you early-access invitations and related updates about PureAir. The legal basis is your explicit consent (GDPR Art. 6(1)(a)), given when you tick the box on the waitlist form. Every email we send is a direct marketing message and carries a one-click way to opt out.

How long we keep it

  • Your active waitlist entry is kept until you register, withdraw, or for up to 3 years from your last contact, whichever comes first.
  • The minimal proof-of-consent record (a hashed identifier plus consent/withdrawal timestamps and the policy version) is archived for the statutory limitation period as evidence that consent was validly obtained — then permanently deleted.

Who we share it with

Your email is processed by our email delivery provider solely to send the invitations you asked for. We do not sell your data or use it for any other purpose.

Your rights

  • Withdraw consent / unsubscribe at any time — one click in any email, as easy as giving it.
  • Access, rectify, or erase your data, and request a copy.
  • Once you withdraw or are erased, your email is added to a do-not-contact suppression list (kept as a one-way keyed hash) so we never accidentally re-add you.

Cookies & tracking

Cookie and tracker consent is handled separately by the cookie banner on the landing page; you can change those choices any time via “Cookie settings”.
